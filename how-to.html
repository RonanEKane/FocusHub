How to Use FocusHub

1. Choose Your Energy Level

Before starting any sprint, pick Low (15 min), Medium (20 min), or High (30 min). Be honest. Your brain knows the difference between "I slept great" and "third coffee trying to function."

Pro tip: Starting with Low energy but feeling great after? Upgrade mid-day. Claiming High when you're dragging? You'll crash. The system works when you're honest.

2. Set Your Baseline Sprint Target

This is your minimum daily commitment. Default is 5 sprints. Tasks will add to this based on priority. Think of it as your floor, not your ceiling.

Pro tip: Start conservative. Better to exceed 5 sprints than fail at 10. Build momentum with wins, not guilt from missing targets.

3. Brain Dump Everything

Type tasks into the Brain Dump. One at a time or paste a list. Don't organize yet. Just capture. The Holding Area is your external hard drive.

Pro tip: Dump first, decide later. If it's in your head taking up space, it goes in the dump. Sorting comes after capture.

4. Sort Into Priority Buckets

Drag tasks from Holding into three buckets:

  • üî• Urgent (1 sprint): Quick admin, emails, calls
  • üéØ Deep Work (2 sprints): Writing, coding, focused creation
  • ‚ôüÔ∏è Strategic (3 sprints): Big moves, hard problems, avoidance work

Watch your sprint target increase automatically as you add tasks.

Pro tip: Most things are 1-2 sprints. If it's 5+ sprints, break it into smaller tasks. Your brain needs wins, not mountains.

5. Run Your First Sprint

Pick a task. Hit the timer button for your energy level. Work until it beeps. No task-switching. No "quick checks." Focus means focus.

Pro tip: Timer runs even if you switch tabs. That's intentional. You can't hide from the clock. Finish the sprint or log the distraction. No middle ground.

6. Log Distractions Immediately

Phone buzz? Coworker interrupt? Thought spiral about dinner? Type it in Distraction Parking. Check "I'll handle this" if you plan to deal with it. This feeds the Intention vs Reality tracker.

Pro tip: Patterns emerge fast. Three "Slack" distractions by 11am? That's not random. That's a signal. The parking lot shows you where your focus leaks.

7. Choose Your Tough Love Level

The AI Agent gives real-time feedback based on your progress. Pick your coaching style:

  • Supportive: Encouraging, gentle nudges
  • Balanced: Realistic assessment, no coddling
  • Tough: Blunt truth, calls out patterns
Pro tip: Try all three. Some days you need support. Other days you need someone to tell you to stop scrolling and get to work.

8. Track Your Progress

Today's Progress card shows sprints completed, tasks done, and distractions logged. The Current Grade updates in real-time based on your performance.

Pro tip: Grade drops with distractions (5% penalty each). Three distractions = 15% grade drop. The system doesn't lie about where your focus went.

9. Use Meeting Tracker

Click "Meeting" button when you enter a meeting. Clock starts. Click again when done. Meeting time is tracked separately from sprint time.

Pro tip: If you're in 4 hours of meetings, your sprint target shouldn't be 8. The system tracks meeting reality so you can plan accordingly.

10. Grade Your Day (End of Day)

Hit END DAY when you're done. System shows its grade. You choose yours using letter buttons (A+, A, A-, B+, B, etc.). Write a reflection if you want. Then complete the day.

Pro tip: One C doesn't matter. Five Cs in a row is a pattern. The Mission Control dashboard shows your weekly rhythm. Use it to spot slips before they become spirals.

11. Check Mission Control Dashboard

Visit the home page to see your cumulative stats: total sprints, day streak (B+ or better), performance index, and Intention vs Reality gap.

Pro tip: Weekly chart shows your consistency. One bad day in a streak of good ones? You're fine. Three bad days in a row? Time to reassess your baseline or task estimates.

12. Complete Tasks, Track Wins

Check the box on any task to complete it. It moves to Today's Wins automatically. Visual proof you're not failing, even when it feels like it.

Pro tip: Finished 2 tasks but feel like you did nothing? Check your wins list. Your brain lies about productivity. The list doesn't.
